**Visual Guidance:** Comparison table with the recommended option column highlighted. Use colour or bold to draw the eye to the recommendation. Avoid more than 4 options — consolidate if needed.

**Speaker Notes:**
{Present the problem statement first — what decision is needed and why now. Walk through each option briefly, noting trade-offs. Explain the recommendation rationale in 2–3 sentences, focusing on why it best balances cost, risk, and strategic alignment. Anticipate likely committee questions and prepare responses.}

> Note: Include this slide only when the committee is asked to choose between alternatives. Remove if not applicable.

## Slide 8: Risk Summary
**Key Message:** {Risk posture — e.g., "Two risks require committee attention; mitigations are in progress with named owners"}

**Content:**

| ID | Risk | L × I | RAG | Mitigation | Owner | Due Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | {risk description} | {H×H} | {🔴} | {mitigation action} | {name} | {DD-MMM-YYYY} |
| R2 | {risk description} | {M×H} | {🟡} | {mitigation action} | {name} | {DD-MMM-YYYY} |
| R3 | {risk description} | {L×M} | {🟢} | {mitigation action} | {name} | {DD-MMM-YYYY} |

**Visual Guidance:** Risk register table as shown. Optionally add a 3×3 heat map (likelihood × impact) with risk IDs plotted. Highlight risks that are new or have escalated since the last update.

**Speaker Notes:**
{Focus on Red and Amber risks only — Green risks need no airtime unless the committee asks. For each elevated risk, state: what could go wrong, what the impact would be, what is being done about it, and who owns the mitigation. If any risk has materialised, state the actual impact and response taken. Note any risks that have been retired since the last update.}

## Slide 9: Decisions Required
**Key Message:** {What the committee is being asked to do — approve, endorse, or note}

**Content:**
1. **The Steering Committee is asked to approve** {specific decision — e.g., "additional $150K investment for Databricks capacity to support streaming ingestion for EDP001."}
2. **The Steering Committee is asked to endorse** {specific direction — e.g., "the recommended Option B for Transcat re-architecture, with delivery targeted for Q3 FY26."}
3. **The Steering Committee is asked to note** {information item — e.g., "the revised delivery timeline for EDP006 Phase 2, now targeting 30-Jun-2026."}

**Visual Guidance:** Numbered list with bold action verbs (approve, endorse, note). No charts or tables — this slide shall be unambiguous text. Use colour or icons to distinguish decision types if needed.

**Speaker Notes:**
{Read each decision item clearly. For approval items, state what happens if approved and what happens if deferred. For endorsement items, confirm that the recommended option was presented earlier. For noting items, confirm that no action is required. Pause after each item to allow committee discussion. Be prepared with supporting detail from earlier slides.}

## Usage Notes

- **Slide count:** This template contains 11 slides (approximately 22 minutes of presentation time). Add or remove slides to match the allocated duration.
- **Optional slides:** Slide 6 (Options / Decision Analysis) should be removed if no decision between alternatives is required.
- **Appendix slides:** Add as many appendix slides as needed — they are reference material, not presented.
- **Classification:** Every slide shall carry the data classification in the footer.
- **Slide numbers:** Every slide shall display its number in the footer.
- **Branding:** The PM shall apply the Westpac Group corporate PowerPoint template.
