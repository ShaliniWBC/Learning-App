## 5. Conversation Starters by Stakeholder

| Stakeholder | Category | Tailored Opening | Value Offered | Follow-up Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | New Leadership | "I lead the Cortex data platform that powers {capability}. I'd welcome 15 minutes to understand your priorities so we can align our roadmap." | {Specific Cortex insight} | {Book meeting / send brief} |
| | Architect | "We're planning the next phase of {initiative}. Your architectural perspective would be valuable — could we schedule a walkthrough?" | {Technical alignment} | {Schedule review} |
| | Business Stakeholder | "I noticed your team is working on {initiative}. Cortex has {capability} that might help — happy to share what worked for {similar team}." | {Relevant data or capability} | {Send summary / demo invite} |
| | Data Science | "The team has delivered {milestone}. I wanted to share the impact metrics and discuss how we can replicate this for {next initiative}." | {Impact metrics} | {Share results / propose collaboration} |
| | Sponsor (Jeni) | "I've prepared a brief for the upcoming {forum}. It includes talking points and the latest Cortex metrics — should save you preparation time." | {Sponsor pack} | {Deliver pack / adjust emphasis} |

### Approaches to Avoid

- ~~"I'd like to introduce myself and tell you about what I do"~~ — centres on Shalini, not the stakeholder
- ~~"Can I get 30 minutes to pick your brain?"~~ — vague, no value offered
- ~~"I'd love your mentorship"~~ — premature for new relationships

## Quality Checklist

Before submitting any cultivation plan or sponsor pack, verify:

- [ ] Cultivation Map relationship states are current
- [ ] 90-Day Plan focuses on 3-5 stakeholders maximum
- [ ] Sponsor Pack includes quantified highlights, not vague claims
- [ ] Talking points are ready-to-use — sponsor should not need to rewrite
- [ ] "Where Shalini Should Be in the Room" entries framed as business need
- [ ] Conversation starters open with stakeholder's needs, not Shalini's
- [ ] No self-promotion language in any artefact
- [ ] All dates use DD-MMM-YYYY format
- [ ] All commitments have explicit owners and due dates
- [ ] Language is professional — no startup jargon or colloquialisms
